The Impact of Social Media on Mental Health

Research Paper Submitted for Psychology 201 - Spring 2025

Abstract

This paper explores the growing concern of social media usage and its potential impacts on mental health, particularly among teenagers and young adults. The research examines various psychological effects, such as increased anxiety, depression, and social comparison, as well as potential benefits such as improved social connection and support networks.

Research Methods

The study utilized a mixed-methods approach, combining surveys to gather quantitative data and interviews to collect qualitative insights from 200 participants aged 18â€“25. Data were analyzed to identify patterns between social media usage and mental health outcomes.

Results

The results showed a significant correlation between heavy social media usage and increased levels of anxiety and depression. However, the study also found that social media usage provided emotional support for a minority of users, suggesting a complex relationship between the two.

Conclusion

In conclusion, while social media has undeniable negative effects on mental health, it is important to recognize its potential positive aspects. Future research should focus on strategies to mitigate the risks while enhancing the benefits, such as promoting healthy usage patterns and improving digital literacy.
